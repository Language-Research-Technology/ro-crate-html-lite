Description Our oral history collection preserves a series of recorded interviews capturing the memories and reflections of individuals connected to the history of our school in 1961. The collection contains approximately 60 items including interviews and recordings of events such as the opening of the school.

Collection characteristics

Purpose Conducted by various interviewers, these conversations explore a wide range of topics, offering personal insights into school life, community connections, and the experiences that shaped the early years of our institution. Together, they provide a valuable and diverse record of voices that bring the past to life. The collection also highlights the school's evolution from its original foundation as the Peninsula Church of England School to what is now known as Peninsula Grammar, ensuring that its rich heritage is remembered and shared with future generations.
