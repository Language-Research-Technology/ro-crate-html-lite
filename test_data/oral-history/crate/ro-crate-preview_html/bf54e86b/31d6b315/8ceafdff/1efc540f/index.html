Description These oral histories are part of a thesis "THE BREATH OF THE WOK: MELBOURNE’S EARLY CHINESE RESTAURANTS" that explores Melbourne’s Chinese restaurants and their relationship with the city from the late 1900s to the early 1950s. Apart from acknowledgement and commentary in historical recipe books by Chinese Australians, these businesses are dismissed in a line or two whenever new culinary and general histories appear. Recorded as socially isolated and frequented by prostitutes, gamblers, drunks and other unrepresentative social groups, they are recorded as ‘uncomfortable’ places for respectable non-Chinese Melburnians to patronise. Despite almost continuous growth from the late nineteenth century, they do not gain currency until the 1950s when they come to the fore as part of the post-war socio-cultural and economic success story.
