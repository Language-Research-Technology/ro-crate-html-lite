Description Our oral history collection is in three parts: 1. A set of approximately 30 audio cassette tapes, which are recordings of interviews and informal discussions conducted with Linton residents or former residents during the 1980s and early 1990s. 2. A set of approximately 17 CD recordings of interviews with Linton residents or former residents, conducted as part of PhD research undertaken by LDHS President Jill Wheeler, in 2005 and 2006. Jill donated these recordings to the Historical Society after she completed her research. 3. Approximately 12 audio-visual recordings on DVDs. These are recordings of talks given at LDHS 'History Nights' between 2012 and 2022. Speakers were all Linton residents or former residents, who outlined their particular family's history in Linton township and district, and recalled aspects of life in Linton as they were growing up.

Collection characteristics

Purpose The Linton & District Historical Society was established in 1981, and motivation for the original interviews was the realisation that many Linton 'identities' had knowledge and stories to tell that would be lost if they were not documented or recorded. Later interviews were conducted as part of research, and talks given at LDHS 'History Nights' were filmed as a means of preserving information presented.

Outputs Occasionally information from the recordings is used in articles in our Society's newsletter, 'Society Notes'. e.g. information from one of the DVDs was used in the article "Another eyewitness account of adventures on the Linton school train", in Society Notes no. 316, August 2022. The same issue of SN also had an article on a Linton 'identity', Tom Andrews (1879-1937). Some information in this article had been confirmed by listening to an interview on one of the CDs.
Other information Since we began to listen to and summarise the contents of these recordings (we have done this intermittently since about 2018) they are proving invaluable as a source of information about Linton's people and history, often providing new stories, and confirming information established through other research.
