Description The Local History Archive collects materials that document the history of the City of Casey region, including, butnot limited to, the councils and their predecessors, from the earliest records of knowledge. The archive has one sub-collection: the reference collection of published works, alongside thousands of unpublished works. The materials held in the archive have enduring, permanent significance and are used extensively by Local History Librarians to develop programs and displays, respond to enquiries and advise local organisations and councils on historical and heritage matters.

Collection characteristics

Purpose The archive itself began as a repository from the former City of Berwick in 1990 and was subsequently handed over to Casey Cardinia Libraries Corporation (predecessor of Connected Libraries) in 1994 following the Council’s restructure. The local history archive acquires, manages and preserves objects relating to the past, present and future of the areas encompassed by the current City of Casey (including neighbouring areas), their communities, notable families and individuals, businesses, associations and organisations.
