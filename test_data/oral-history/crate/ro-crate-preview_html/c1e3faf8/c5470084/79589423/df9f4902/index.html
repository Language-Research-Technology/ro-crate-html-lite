Description The Federation of Community Legal Centres wanted to document the history of the Victorian community legal sector movement through the words of those who were there at the beginning. This movement for justice, which now includes 50 community legal centres across the state, began in the 1970s, when a small group of forward-thinking lawyers established the first community legal centre in Victoria, in an effort to address unmet legal need and to create an organisation through which they could advocate for just and fair laws. Last year, we invited some of the founding members of the sector/movement to speak freely about that time and their experiences in the nascent years of the community legal sector. The conversation was chaired by Jon Faine AM, who spent time working in community legal centres in the early 80s. The conversation lasted one hour and the audio was recorded.
