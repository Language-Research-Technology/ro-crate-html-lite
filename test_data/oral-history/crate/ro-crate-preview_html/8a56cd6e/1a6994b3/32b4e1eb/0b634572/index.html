Brunswick Oral History Project (For a Better Life we came) 1984/85

Collection details

Collection name Brunswick Oral History Project (For a Better Life we came) 1984/85
Collection holder Merri-bek Libraries/Merri-bek Council
Contributor Tonya Lewis
Rights holder Merri-bek Libraries/Merri-bek Council
Publisher Merri-bek Libraries/Merri-bek Council
Accountable person Tonya Lewis (Adult/Information Services Librarian)
Language Italian and Greek
Location Brunswick
Date Recording period: 1980s; Historical eras discussed: 1920s, 1930s, 1940s, 1950s, 1960s, 1970s
Description Series of 50 oral history interviews undertaken with Greek and Italian migrants to Brunswick. Excerpts of around 20 interviews formed a book "For a better life we came" (1985) and travelling exhibition with some photographs.

Collection characteristics

Purpose Document the process of 1950s/1960s migration and settlement and effect on the municipality.
Format (audio/visual) Audio-only
Analogue or digital Analogue-only
Audio format Compact Cassette Tapes
Audiovisual format Other: No AV interviews
Number of interviews 50
Total hours 80
Average interview length 1.5 hours

Finding aids & access

Transcripts Some interviews have transcripts
Related materials Photographs of interviewees
Catalogue / finding aid Partially
Outputs Edited excerpts (in Greek/Italian) and English translations from some interviews together with photos of some families created travelling exhibition and book 'For a better life we came' (1985).
Other information Currently analogue tapes only. Intention to digitize 2025. Introduction in book suggests some transcripts/translations available. Blank permission forms suggested intention was that recordings be available for 'bona fide scholars'. Blank permission form also reads 'No other use of the material may be made without my written permission during my lifetime, after which the libraries may use the material as they see fit'. However, only one completed form has been found in two boxes of project-related material in LH stack.
