Description AUDIO TAPES: 31 audio cassette taped recordings of speakers on a wide variety of subjects relating to Phillip Island. VIDEO TAPES: 12 on various topics, but some without sound. 3MFM RECORDINGS: Radio 3MFM (now Coastal radio) local history series of talks by Phillip Island residents. Uploaded onto Soundcloud 'pidhs' account: https://soundcloud.com/you/tracks. PIDHS was given CDs of all of these by the radio station. DR ANDREA CLELAND and DR REBECCA SANDERS INTERVIEWS: 9 interviews. Digital copies of all of Dr Cleland's interviews plus typed transcripts were shared with the interview subjects and edited at their request. Essays from the transcripts and images interviewees and of their families provided, which are in the pdf book 'Phillip Island Life 1940-2022' on the PIDHS website. AUSTRALIA REMEMBERS WWII There are also several versions of the Australia Remembers videos, where locals are sharing their wartime experiences. Plus a series of YouTube videos put together based on their own words, and one of POW Snow Dixon speaking directly to the camera of his war and POW experiences. https://www.youtube.com/@phillipislanddistricthisto9047

Collection characteristics

Purpose To preserve information and memories from older and knowledgeable individuals before this knowledge was lost. To use the recorded information in exhibitions and presentations, including YouTube videos. To share the transcripts and or any form of publications derived from them as widely as possible.

Outputs Radio program: Coastal Radio South Gippsland/Bass Coast Phillip Island local history series, 2016. Included in two books of historical talks and information available as free download pdfs from the society's website: 'Phillip Island History and Memories' and 'Phillip Island Life: 1920 - 2022'. YouTube video series about Phillip Island during WWII and experiences of some local WWII servicemen, chicory growing, wreck of the SS Speke poem based on eye-witness accounts. Used by the local newspaper Phillip Island & San Remo Advertiser for various articles, and in their book: 'Once Upon and Island'.
