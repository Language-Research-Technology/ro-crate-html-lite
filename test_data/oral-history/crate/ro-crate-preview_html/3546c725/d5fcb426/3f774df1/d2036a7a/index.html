Description Interviews of past students, parents and teachers at Mordialloc Primary School recorded on cassettes in 1993, beginning with the then oldest surviving student 90 year old Alf Groves and ending with an interview of the first female Principal Joy Gale 1975-1981. The cut off date coincided with the first Federal Government money given to public primary schools by the Whitlam Government. Questions related to Mordialloc Primary School and life at particular times. Each person signed a release that the interviews could be used for educational purposes to preserve the history of the school.

Collection characteristics

Purpose I was a volunteer parent who initiated the project. (I have a double history major from ANU in 1970s). I attended an oral history workshop at Benga House, Heritage Hill Museum Dandenong to get some tips, bought the cassette tapes and borrowed a microphone from a friend plus a tape recorder with foot control for editing. I have original raw tapes plus edited ones used for the school's 125th Celebration where I set up listening posts in the school hall for people to listen to the recordings. I applied for funding to do more with the tapes, perhaps publish a book but no one was interested. However, I think the tapes are historically important and should be kept and made available for anyone interested and nowadays they need to be digital. I need help to digitise and also want to be sure they will be valued and looked after.

Other information Oral history was a particular interest of mine and I also have some selected interviews with friends and family. However the series for Mordialloc Primary's 125th Celebration is an important historical collection with perspectives from students, parents, cleaners, teachers ,and principals with the World Wars and Depression mentioned, polio epidemic, transition from horse to car, encounters with Pharlap (Mordialloc Primary had special dispensation to keep students to 14 years of age because those wanting to be jockeys did not intend going to high school.) At the time Mordialloc Primary was under threat of closure by the Kennett Government, I was told anything I did had to be 'cost negative' therefore I paid for all the cassettes, photographs, developing and photocopying etc.
