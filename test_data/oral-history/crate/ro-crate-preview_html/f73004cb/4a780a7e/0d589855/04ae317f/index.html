Description A large collection of local newspapers , many on microfilm, newspaper cuttings which form family files, rate books, sporting files, maps, some farming artefacts, an authentic shepherd's hut, War memorabilia, some clothing, approx 2500 photos which have been accessioned and none of the collection has been digitised as we have few members and all are volunteers and belong to several other voluntary organisations as well. We have several family histories and also some dvd's, tapes and a few films for which we lack equipment to show. All our members are in 60's to 80's.

Collection characteristics

Purpose To preserve the history of the town and schools and small communities around Birchip for future generations. We were able to eventually purchase and do up an old courthouse after a town centenary in 1982 when a big display of farming items and photos was held and people realised that it all needed to be collated. We try to work with students from the P12 school when we can. We do lots of research for people which brings in a little money but is very time consuming. Internet has made people research from home. Privacy rules are a bit of an issue. We have a segment from a century ago in our local paper which we began during Covid. We are not able to go to seminars etc in Melbourne so rely on online sessions. We try to have new themed displays for our town festival each year, occasional Open days and open by appointment.
