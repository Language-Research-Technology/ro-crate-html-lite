Description The collection is a series of cassette recordings of a 30 minute program called 'Time and Place' that went to air on a Monday night between 9.00 and 9.30 on Radio 3RRR. It ran between 1985 and 1987 having grown out of a program called 'The History Show' which moved from 3RRR to 3CR during 1985. Time and Place was less academic in style and presentation than 'The History Show' and it included interviews with authors from a range of backgrounds, reviews of books and magazines, discussions of current issues in history education, archive and heritage preservation and short interviews connected with the oral history of places and sites in Melbourne. The quality of each show varied considerably but it allegedly out-rated ABC radio on a Monday night in the 9.00-9.30 time slot. The tapes are generally in good condition and some have been digitised. Aside from significant interviews the tapes also provide an insight into an aspect of public radio in Melbourne during the 1980s.

Collection characteristics

Purpose The shows were taped so we could review what worked and what didn't. None of us had any training in radio presentation or production so each programme was a learning experience.

Other information My intention has been to write an article about the programme with discussion about some of the more memorable interviews and segments. I am unsure of how or if copyright is relevant as all the programmes are live broadcasts. Programmes were put together quickly each week and as this was live radio it captures a style of presentation that is lost when something is pre-recorded or presented as a podcast – or when one of the presenters is also not the show's producer. We had a deliberate focus to make the segments widely accessible and we often cited the old adage about radio being the theatre of the mind so we tried to avoid the style of broadcast that was a lecture on the radio.
