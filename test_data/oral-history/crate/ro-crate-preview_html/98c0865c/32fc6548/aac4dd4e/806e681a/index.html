Description It is a collection of 21 interviews with mainly the women who were the subjects or witnesses in my PhD thesis, which was a history of women teachers in the Education Department of Victoria over time (1880s to 1980s). They were chosen as they added to Victoria's social capital, as they were also feminists, fought for equal pay, pioneering principals and inspectors, first women Ministers of Education, politicians, one of whom was Joan Kirner for her leadership of parents organisations and the first women premier, unionists, exchange teachers, patriotic fund raisers, physical education leaders, curriculum writers, pioneering teachers college principal, and domestic arts teachers. Others were involved in the creation of the Australian Bicentenary's 1988 Business and Professional Women's Stained Glass Window, which I was commissioned to write about. I also interviewed women politicians for a film, made in 1988. They have been edited and are on a video, Women in Parliament (1988), that I also directed. It was funded by the Australian Bicentenary Program.
