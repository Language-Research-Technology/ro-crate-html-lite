Description About 240 taped presentations/talks given at local history reminiscences group in Brunswick that met mainly in 1980s and 1990s. Topics range from early days of European activity in Brunswick 1850s to early 2000s. Informal group led/facilitated by Brunswick Library staff member. Many pertain to first half of twentieth century. Mix of personal memories and other local research by speakers. There was often a presentation followed by questions/discussion. Some tapes are shared memories by group on a particular topic. Around 44 of the talks are given by local historian, Les Barnes who authored many local histories. Around 25 were given by Marjorie Glew. Range of other speakers with strong links to the local community.

Other information Collection is sometimes referred to as Talks on Brunswick or Talks on Moreland (later name after Council amalgamations). There is an index to the majority of the collection converted from DB Textworks to Excel on organisational Content Management (Records) system. (227 of 240 recordings). There is also a list of titles of 240 digital recordings (.Wav) on shared organisational network drive. Timed summaries for around 35 topics/tapes were created as Work from Home staff project during Covid pandemic. They were sometimes created by staff with limited knowledge of content/context and may include some inaccuracies. Timed summaries are currently located on organisational Content Management system.
